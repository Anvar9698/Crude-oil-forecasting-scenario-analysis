## 📌 Project Overview
This project focuses on **Exponential Triple Smoothing (ETS) forecasting** and **Scenario Analysis** using Excel, applied to the case of *Nandani Petroleum & Energy*, a global energy company highly dependent on crude oil exports.  

The analysis leverages historical **West Texas Intermediate (WTI) crude oil price data (1946–2025)** to forecast prices up to **2050** and evaluates how different market scenarios could impact the company’s financial performance.

---

## 🎯 Objectives
- Forecast WTI crude oil prices up to 2050 using Excel’s ETS forecasting tool.
- Identify long-term trends, seasonality, and volatility in crude oil prices.
- Conduct scenario analysis (optimistic vs. pessimistic) to assess revenue and profit impacts.
- Provide actionable insights for business decision-making under uncertainty.

---

## 🛠 Methodology
### Step 1: Forecasting
- Used Excel’s **Forecast Sheet** with ETS algorithm.
- Confidence interval: **95%**.
- Generated future crude oil price projections until 2050.

### Step 2: Data Analysis
- Examined forecasted trends and seasonality.
- Highlighted potential fluctuations in oil prices.

### Step 3: Financial Calculations
- Linked forecasted prices to company revenue calculations via **VLOOKUP**.
- Adjusted units sold based on scenario assumptions.

### Step 4: Scenario Analysis
- Applied **Scenario Manager (What-If Analysis)** in Excel.
- Defined two scenarios:
  - **Pessimistic (U.S. EV Policy Shift)** → Decline in oil demand & prices.
  - **Optimistic (Geopolitical Conflict)** → Surge in oil prices & revenue.
- Changing cells: `Refined Petroleum Sales Revenue` & `Material Expenses`.
- Result cells: `Total Revenue` & `Net Profit`.

---

## 📊 Key Insights
- **Pessimistic Scenario**: Significant revenue decline due to reduced U.S. demand for fossil fuels.
- **Optimistic Scenario**: Revenue and profit increase driven by geopolitical supply shocks.
- **Forecast**: Long-term crude oil prices show cyclical volatility but overall upward pressure under supply constraints.
